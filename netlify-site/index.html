PR #54 · v1.7.0 · REVIEW GUIDE

More room to roam. Less friction on the way.

Player and pet menus are now distinct, nested shops are easier to navigate, scene zoom follows you, and residents can wander and chat beyond Town — without the interaction slowdown.

Player-facing highlights

What changed

I

Your inventory

I opens player clothes, food, and furniture. P is reserved for pet chat, feeding, and pet clothes.

←

Menus that remember

Back rows return to the parent menu, pagination is clearer, and toggling clothes keeps the active page and row selected.

⌕

Zoom follows you

Choose a camera zoom once and it remains at that level when moving between Town, Shore, shops, House, and both Greens.

…

Conversations travel

Shore, West Green, and East Green residents use the full dialogue flow instead of Town being the only place to chat.

Investigation result

Why approaching objects lagged

!

The proximity glow was a GPU post-processing pass

Every interactable enabled Phaser glow just as the player entered range. That expensive effect caused the visible stall. The interaction cue is now a lightweight warm tint, retaining feedback without adding a post-FX pass.

Glow post-FX→Warm tint
Scene continuity

Residents keep their place

Unique assignments

Shore and both Green scenes reserve distinct residents. Town excludes those reservations, so one NPC cannot appear in two places at once.

Persistent positions

Each non-town resident’s identity and roaming position are restored when the player leaves and later returns during the game session.

  1. Check the splitIn any hub, press I for player inventory and P for pet care. Open clothes and use Back to return through nested menus.
  2. Cross a sceneSet a distinctive zoom, then move from Town to Shore or either Green. Confirm the same zoom and approach an object without the old glow stall.
  3. Meet residentsTalk at Shore and both Greens. Revisit a scene to confirm its resident and position persist, with no duplicate resident in Town.
